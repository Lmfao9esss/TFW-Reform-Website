Youth Unemployment vs Labour Supply

youth unemployment in Canada is normally higher than the national average. When labour supply increases in entry-level fields it can make more competition for high school students looking for part time jobs.

If entry level job markets get bigger through temporary labour programs while youth unemployment is still high, wage growth might slow down and youth employment could decline.
From Statistics Canada: Canada’s population grew by over 1 million people in 2023 (record growth). Youth unemployment (ages 15–24) is typically higher than the national average. Housing supply has not matched population growth in major cities. From Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada: Canada increased temporary resident targets significantly in recent years. Labour Market Impact Assessments (LMIAs) approve employers to hire foreign workers when shortages are proven.
Statistics Canada reports that Canada's population has grown at a record rate in recent years, placing higher demands on housing and services. High school student unemployment, however, continues to be higher than the national average.
