Growth of Low-Skill TFWs

The rise in low skill TFW permits allows businesses to employ foreign workers in entry level, retail, and food service industries.

Employers may stop hiring Canadian high school students and delay wage increases if they depend on cheaper foreign labor.

In addition to decreasing high school students' opportunities and slowing wage growth, this can also increase reliance on cheap labor rather than innovation.

Who Does This Affect?

This issue affects high school students, new graduates, and young adults in Ottawa who are having a hard time trying to find entry level employment.

Why Is This a Problem?

Youth unemployment has increased while the growth of our population has sped up. Entry level jobs are becoming more competitive, making it harder for students to get experience.

Why Should People Care?

Youth employment builds independence, financial stability, and workforce skills. If young people cant get jobs, it impacts the future economy and community in ottawa.
