Why Some Support Expanding Low-Skill Temporary Foreign Workers

Argument 1: Real Labour Shortages Exist

People in support of issuing more low skilled temporary foreign worker (TFW) permits might argue that lots of companies face real labour shortages. Fields such as agriculture, food service, hospitality, and construction often report difficulty filling positions with local workers.

From this perspective, limiting low skilled TFW permits could hurt businesses, reduce productivity, and increase prices for customers.

Argument 2: Regional and Rural Challenges

In small towns and rural areas employers might argue that its hard to find people to work low skill/low wage jobs because of the decrease of population in those areas and the rise in old generations.

Argument 3: Economic Growth and Flexibility

According to some economists, increasing the amount of foreign labor helps overall economic development and helps control inflation. Temporary workers can offer flexibility to businesses that experience fast growth.​

Response: A Balanced Reform Approach

While labour shortages and regional challenges are very real, relying on low cost tfws can discourage wage growth, investing in automation and productivity.

A better policy should demand more proof of labor shortages push towards wage increases before permits are approved, and prioritize high skill fields that contribute alot more to society.

The goal of reform is not to get rid of tfws, but to make immegration more consistant with our countrys job market, housing supply, infrastructure and long term economic stability.
