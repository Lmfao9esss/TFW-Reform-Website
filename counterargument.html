Why Some Support Expanding Low-Skill Temporary Foreign Workers

Argument 1: Real Labour Shortages Exist

Supporters of expanding low-skill Temporary Foreign Worker (TFW) permits argue that many industries face genuine labour shortages. Sectors such as agriculture, food service, hospitality, and construction often report difficulty filling positions with domestic workers.

From this perspective, limiting low-skill TFW permits could hurt businesses, reduce productivity, and increase prices for consumers.

Argument 2: Regional and Rural Challenges

In smaller communities and rural regions, population decline and aging demographics make it harder to attract local workers. Employers in these areas argue that foreign workers are essential to keeping businesses open and supporting local economies.

Argument 3: Economic Growth and Flexibility

Some economists argue that increasing labour supply helps control inflation and supports overall economic growth. Temporary workers provide flexibility during periods of rapid expansion.

Response: A Balanced Reform Approach

While labour shortages and regional challenges are real, long-term reliance on low-cost temporary labour may discourage wage growth, investment in automation, and productivity improvements.

A sustainable policy should require stronger proof of shortages, encourage wage increases before permit approvals, and prioritize high-skill sectors that contribute more significantly to innovation and tax revenue.

The goal of reform is not to eliminate temporary foreign workers, but to align immigration policy with infrastructure capacity, housing supply, and long-term economic stability.
